## About The Project

This study investigates the performances of the YOLOv5 and Resnet50 when applied to a video composed of multiple frames with various forms of distortions. The research question is to analyze the impact of these distortions on object detection algorithmsâ€™ performance.
To answer this question, we used the YOLOv5 architecture (Pre-trained on COCO dataset) and the Resnet50 architecture (pretrained on ImageNet) in order to test their accuracy on low quality video. Our analysis shows that the algorithm's accuracy is indeed impacted by the degree and type of image corruption, with certain forms of distortion leading to a marked decrease in performance. 
Overall, our study concludes that while the YOLOv5 and Resnet50 algorithms are effective for object detection in clear images and not effective when images are too corrupted.This is why additional steps may be necessary to ensure its accuracy in corrupted images.

## Usage

 -  ```Resnet_on_Tiny_Imagenet.ipynb ```: Testing ResNet50 on augmented data (ImageNet) with transformations that degrates the images consequently.
 -  ```YoloV5_Inference_on_Augmentation_Dataset_MsCOCO.ipynb ```: Testing YoloV5 on augmented data(COCO) with transformations that degrates the images consequently.
 - ```inference_resnet50_on-video.ipynb```: Using ResNet50 on a video.
 - ```TrainingResNetOnMNIST.```:  Training ResNet18 on the MNIST database and showing feature maps of a MNIST image.
